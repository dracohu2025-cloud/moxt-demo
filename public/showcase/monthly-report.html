Executive Summary

Looking ahead to April's contract renewal — we're preparing a Q1 retrospective and Q2 growth roadmap to present at our March 15 QBR.

Top 3 Wins
MQL record broken. 284 MQLs is an all-time monthly high, driven by a 40% increase in Google Ads quality score and 3 new blog articles ranking page 1.
Cost Per MQL down 12%. Audience segmentation improvements reduced waste spend on non-ICP traffic. CPM dropped $8 on LinkedIn.
SEO momentum building. "B2B workflow automation" reached position 5 (was 12 in November), driving 44 organic MQLs — up from 0 six months ago.
Top 3 Issues
SQL conversion rate flat at 18%. Despite MQL growth, the volume of sales-accepted leads has not grown proportionally. Root cause: possible MQL qualification drift — working with your sales team on criteria alignment.
Email nurture engagement declining. Open rates dropped from 28% to 22% in February. Likely list fatigue. Recommend a list hygiene pass and sequence refresh in Q2.
LinkedIn CPC increased 18% vs Jan. Platform-wide CPC inflation in B2B tech vertical. Testing new ad formats (thought leader ads) to counteract. Early results promising.
March Priorities
MQL scoring alignment session with your sales ops team (week of Mar 17). Goal: tighten ICP definition to improve SQL conversion rate from 18% → 25% by April.
Launch LinkedIn Thought Leader Ads featuring your CEO. Early tests show 40% lower CPL vs standard sponsored content. Full rollout budget: $4K in March.
Q1 retrospective + Q2 roadmap deck ready for Mar 15 QBR. We'll present a renewed strategy with expanded channel coverage for Q2 and renewal options.
